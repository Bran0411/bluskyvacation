    Must-see experiences

    Nine landscapes.
    One extraordinary journey.

    🏔️
    Northern Altay

    Kanas Lake & The Three Bays

    China's most pristine alpine lake, deep inside the Altay Mountains. Wolong Bay, Fairy Bay, and Moon Bay each have their own character — mist-draped, moon-curved river bends through birch forest. The Guanyu Terrace overlooks it all.

    🌲
    Northern Altay · Autumn Gold

    Hemu Village

    One of China's most beautiful villages. Wooden Tuva houses, white birch forests, and the Hemu River valley. In autumn the birches turn gold — in winter they're cloaked in snow. From the observation deck, the whole village unfolds below you like a painting.

    Summer: birch & wildflowers · Winter: snow village
    🌈
    Northern Altay · Sunset

    Wucai Beach 五彩滩

    A Danxia landscape along the Irtysh River. At sunset, the rock formations glow in shades of red, orange, and yellow — and across the river, green poplars form a sharp contrast. One of the most photogenic spots in all of Xinjiang.

    👻
    Karamay · Yadan Landforms

    Ghost City — Urho

    Also known as the "Wind City." Wind-carved rock pillars of extraordinary shapes rise across the Gobi Desert. When the wind howls between the formations, the sound resembles ghostly cries. A place like nowhere else on Earth.

    💎
    Ili · Any season

    Sayram Lake

    Called "The Last Tear of the Atlantic" — the westernmost ocean-connected lake in China. Crystal blue waters ringed by snow peaks. In summer, swans and wildflowers. In winter, blue ice, icefalls, and ice bubbles that look like another planet.

    Summer: blue mirror · Winter: ice palace
    🐎
    Ili · Seasonal

    Nalati Sky Grassland

    A sub-alpine meadow at nearly 2,000m altitude — rolling green hills, wildflower seas, and snow-capped peaks. Called "Paradise on Earth." On certain months, herds of heavenly horses run through the Zhaosu Wetland below. Shuttle bus from base included.

    Best: May–September
    🛣️
    Seasonal · Jun–Sep

    Duku Highway 独库公路

    One of China's most beautiful roads — 560km through the Tianshan Mountains. The highway passes from alpine snow into grasslands, canyon gorges, and desert in a single day. Literally "four seasons in one day." Seasonal (Jun–Sep); train alternative in other months.

    Seasonal: June–September only
    🌸
    Ili · Seasonal

    Tianshan Flower Sea & Kazanchi Folk Area

    In May–June, peonies and lavender carpet the Tianshan foothills. In August–September, Kazanchi Folk Area opens — ride a horse cart through Uyghur streets, taste the famous Marajun ice cream, and watch Uzbek folk performances.

    Flowers: May–Jun · Culture: Aug–Sep
    ⛷️
    Winter exclusive

    General Mountain Ski Resort + Yunxiao Cable Car

    Winter adds experiences no other season offers: skiing in Altay (some of China's best snow), a snow rave on the mountain, the Yunxiao Peak cable car with views across four countries (China, Russia, Kazakhstan, Mongolia), and a hot spring soak at Shawan.

    Winter only: Nov–Feb

    Xinjiang food culture

    Eating your way across Northern Xinjiang

    🐑
    Roast Whole Lamb 烤全羊
    Farewell banquet on the final night — the most ceremonial meal in Xinjiang
    🍚
    Hand-Grabbed Rice 手抓饭
    The definitive Uyghur dish — lamb, carrots, raisins over slow-cooked rice
    🍗
    Big Plate Chicken 大盘鸡
    Xinjiang's most famous dish — spiced chicken and potato in a wide clay plate
    🐟
    Burqin Cold-Water Fish 冷水鱼
    Clear mountain stream trout from the Irtysh — simply grilled or steamed
    🍢
    Xinjiang BBQ Skewers 烤串
    Lamb skewers from the night market — seasoned with cumin and chilli
    🍖
    Signature Lamb Ribs 羊排
    Braised or grilled — Xinjiang's lamb is uniquely sweet from mountain grass
    🍲
    Hemu Clay Pot Hotpot
    Winter exclusive — warming clay pot with Tuva-style broth in the snow village
    🍜
    Xinjiang Lagman Noodles 拌面
    Hand-pulled noodles with lamb and vegetables — a Uyghur staple
